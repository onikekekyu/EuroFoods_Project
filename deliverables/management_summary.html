Executive summary

EuroFoods grew revenue +5.4% to €2.36M in 2024 (+€122k) while holding gross margin essentially flat at 38.7% — healthy, broad-based growth led by a +11.9% Q3 surge. However, the company finished 4.6% (−€115k) below budget, and critically the miss was uniform across every country and category. A price-volume bridge shows the entire shortfall is volume (−€226k over two years) with price/mix at virtually zero — i.e. the plan over-assumed units, not euros. The story is not weak execution but an over-optimistic top-down budget against a fundamentally sound, profitable business.

Top 3 findings

1

The budget gap is volume, not price

Actuals are ~5% below plan in all 3 countries and all 4 categories (variance band −5.0% to −3.9%). The bridge attributes −€226k to volume, ≈€0 to price. This points to a planning-assumption error, not a commercial failure.

2

Online is the slowest channel, not the fastest

Against the usual e-commerce narrative, Online grew only +4.9% vs brick-and-mortar at +6.0%. Supermarket (+5.9%) is the real engine. Online — 22.6% of revenue — is underperforming its potential.

3

Frozen Food is the structural margin drag

Group margin is steady, but all 5 Frozen Food SKUs sit below their 35% target. It is the largest category by revenue (€1.42M) yet the lowest-margin band — a category-wide sourcing issue, not weak individual products.

Risks & assumptions

  • Budget premise: recommendations assume the uniform ~5% gap is planning optimism, not demand erosion. If volumes are structurally declining, rebaselining masks a real problem — monitor the volume trend monthly.
  • Scenario independence: the Online +20% / Convenience −10% model treats segments as independent; real channel cannibalisation would reduce the +€68k net uplift.
  • Margin & FX stability: the €2.5M / forecast cases hold margin at 38.7% and assume no FX shock. EUR/GBP ranged 0.831–0.883 (~6%) over the period — a weaker pound directly compresses reported UK revenue (27% of the group).
