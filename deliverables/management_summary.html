Executive Summary

EuroFoods delivered €2.36M revenue in 2024, up +5.4% on 2023, with gross margin steady at 38.7% — a healthy, profitable year. However, the company finished −4.6% (−€114.6k) below budget, and the miss was systematic: every country and every category landed ~5% short, driven almost entirely by volume (−56.8k units) rather than price. The headline issue is not performance — it is an over-optimistic plan, compounded by an underperforming Online channel that grew slower than the stores.

! Top 3 Findings

1 · The budget miss is volume, not price — and it is everywhere

A price–volume bridge splits the −€226.5k two-year gap into −€226.2k volume / −€0.3k price. Actuals sit ~5% under plan in all 3 countries and all 4 categories (France −5.0%, Germany −5.0%, UK −5.0%). Uniformity that complete signals a top-down plan set too high, not a local execution failure.

2 · Online is the laggard, not the leader

Against the usual e-commerce narrative, Online grew only +4.9% while brick-and-mortar grew +6.0%. Supermarket (+5.9%) is the real growth engine. Online is now 22.6% of revenue but is diluting the company growth rate.

3 · Frozen Food is the structural margin drag

Group margin is healthy, but all 5 Frozen Food SKUs fall below their 35% target (category ≈ 34.9%). This is a category-wide sourcing/pricing issue, not weak individual products — so the answer is cost & price action, not discontinuation. Beverages is by far the most seasonal line (July index 136 vs February 74).

→ Recommendations

Rebase the 2025 budget on actuals +€115k credibility

Reset targets to a realistic +5–6% volume trajectory off 2024 actuals. This alone converts a chronic −5% "miss" into an achievable plan and restores forecast trust.

Fix or re-cost the Online channel +€50–80k

Online's +4.9% lags the +6.0% stores. Audit pricing, fulfilment cost and assortment; closing the gap to the store rate adds ~€6k/yr directly, and a targeted +20% Online push offsets a −10% Convenience decline for a net +€68k (+2.9%) — the most efficient growth lever available.

Attack Frozen Food cost-to-serve +€15–20k margin

Renegotiate frozen supply or lift price ~1–2% to pull the category back above its 35% target. A 1pp margin recovery on €1.42M frozen revenue ≈ €14k, with no volume risk if paired with seasonality-aware promo.

Risks & Assumptions

  • Flat-price assumption: the scenario holds 2024 prices; input-cost inflation could erode the frozen margin gains.
  • FX exposure: UK is 27% of revenue; EUR/GBP swung 0.831–0.883 (~6%) — translation risk not in these EUR figures.
  • Linear forecast: the projection assumes 2024 seasonality repeats; a demand shock or lost key account (top customers are concentrated) would break it.
